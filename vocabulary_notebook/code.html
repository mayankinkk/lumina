Meticulous

/məˈtɪkjələs/
ADJECTIVE B2 MASTERY

Meaning

Showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise.

Example

"She is meticulous when writing code, ensuring every function is perfectly optimized before deployment."

psychology

Linguistic Insight

Originates from the Latin meticulosus (fearful), from metus (fear). The meaning shifted in the 16th century from 'fearful' to 'fussy about details' to its modern sense of 'careful precision'.
